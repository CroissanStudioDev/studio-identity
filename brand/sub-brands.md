# Sub-brands

Today the studio operates under **two sibling identities** that share the same lockup system,
typeface, and mark. They are not parent/child — Studio doesn't "own" Community. Don't combine
the two wordmarks in one artifact.

## Croissan Studio (commercial)

Default identity. Used for:
- Client work, contracts, invoices, proposals
- Marketing site `croissanstudio.ru`
- Pitch decks, case studies, sales material
- Social posts about commercial projects

Voice: caring, smart, slightly bold. Partner-not-vendor framing.
See [`voice-and-tone.md`](voice-and-tone.md).

## Croissan Community (non-commercial)

Used for:
- Community events, meetups, edu programs
- Open-source projects and public artifacts not tied to commercial work
- Talks, workshops, content where we represent the broader practitioner community

Same lockup system, same three background tiles (blue / white / black), same typeface.
See [`../identity/logo/usage.md`](../identity/logo/usage.md) for lockup specs.

The voice can be a step **less polished and more direct** than Studio — you're talking to peers,
not prospects — but the brand voice principles (concrete numbers, no corporate hedging, no emoji
as icons) still apply.

## Choosing which to use

| If the artifact is… | Use |
|---------------------|-----|
| Client-facing, commercial, paid work | Studio |
| Free, community-facing, peer-facing | Community |
| Internal docs / tools | Studio (default) |
| Mixed (e.g., a meetup sponsored by a client) | Studio, with a Community credit line |

## Don'ts

- Don't combine the two wordmarks in one composition.
- Don't invent a third sub-brand without explicit founder approval.
- Don't apply Studio voice (sales-y CTAs like «Забронировать звонок») to Community artifacts.
- Don't apply Community informality to client-facing material.
